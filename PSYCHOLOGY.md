# LOCKED IN — The Psychology

Why each feature works on the brain. Compiled from a multi-agent research pass
(find → cite → confidence-rate → translate to *this* app), grounded in named
studies. Every claim carries a **confidence** tag: `high` = well-replicated,
`medium` = real but contested/with caveats, `low` = effect plausible but the
specific number/citation is soft (we say so honestly instead of faking it).

Built for one user: a 19-year-old Christian college track athlete (400/800m,
reports to Fresno State in August), in porn recovery, rebuilding a routine.

**The spine of the whole app:** *a reset is data, not failure.* Marlatt's
Abstinence Violation Effect says the cognitive **framing** of a slip — not the
slip — is what turns one lapse into a relapse. Everything below protects that.

## 1 · Habit formation — the morning protocol, tasks, reading plan

> **Top move:** decouple the protocol from a "perfect chain." One missed day is
> noise; the *second* consecutive miss is the risk.

| Principle | Source · confidence | In the app |
|---|---|---|
| **Implementation intentions** (if-then plans), d=.65, biggest on *getting started* | Gollwitzer & Sheeran 2006 · **high** | Rewrite every protocol item / recurring task as a fixed cue→action: *"When I sit down after waking → 15 min prayer/Bible"*, *"When the 10:15 alarm fires → phone out of the bedroom."* Cheapest, highest-leverage copy change in the app. |
| **One missed day barely dents a habit**; the danger is two in a row | Lally et al. 2010 (66 days = median, not a rule) · **high** | Tri-state = DONE / not-yet / **MISSED (neutral grey, never red, never "FAILED")**, no streak-reset animation. Surface *"Twice now. Reset tomorrow."* **only** on a 2nd consecutive miss. Silent after one. |
| **Abstinence Violation Effect** — "I've ruined it" → shame → relapse | Marlatt & Gordon 1985; Larimer 1999 · **high** | The spine of the reset journal. After a reset: *"Logged. The clock restarts; the work doesn't."* Never relapse/failure/broke/gave-in. Keep lifetime WIN + income totals on-screen so day-0 is never the only number. |
| **Self-monitoring is itself the intervention** (stronger when recorded + reported) | Harkin et al. 2016 (138 studies) · **high** | Keep logging frictionless and the artifact visible (calendar, counts). The partner-text = the "public" condition. Don't bury history in menus. |
| **Fresh-start effect** — motivation spikes after temporal landmarks | Dai, Milkman & Riis 2014 · **high** | Sunday Debrief = the re-commit moment: *"New week. One change. Go."* + the Fresno report-date countdown. |
| **Habit stacking / context stability** | Wood & Neal 2007; Keller 2022 · **high** | Keep the protocol a fixed, ordered stack — each item the cue for the next. Anchor reading to a stable cue, not "sometime today." |
| **Identity-based habits** (bidirectional) | Verplanken & Sui 2019 · **medium** | After a clean morning: *"This is who you are now."* (not "good job", no badge). Tie to athlete-and-faith identity, never points. |
| **Friction asymmetry** — make good easier, bad harder | Fogg 2009 (B=MAP); Thaler & Sunstein 2008 · **high** | Phone-out-of-bedroom is the textbook friction-raise on the porn cue. Keep desired actions 1-tap; add zero confirmations to checkoffs / sprint start / urge protocol. |
| **Caveat: rigidity backfires** | van Timmeren & de Wit 2022; Fogg · **medium** | Forgiving on the *clock*, rigid on the *cue* (see grace window). Don't pile on stricter targets than recovery can sustain. |

## 2 · Relapse & urges — streak, urge protocol, reset journal

> **Top move:** make the reset journal the structural firewall against the
> "what-the-hell effect." A reset must **not** zero out the day or week.

| Principle | Source · confidence | In the app |
|---|---|---|
| **What-the-hell / counterregulation** — cross the line, blow past it | Herman & Polivy 1984; Polivy & Herman 2000 · **high** | Never show a "you've already broken it today" state. A 2nd reset = identical neutral copy, no same-day failure tally. |
| **Shame predicts relapse; guilt does not** | Randles & Tracy 2013; Dearing et al. 2005 · **high** | Audit all post-lapse copy for self-as-bad language. Feeling field = *"What state were you in?"* (situational), not "How could you?" For a Christian user, avoid anything reading as moral condemnation. |
| **Self-compassion beats self-criticism** after failure | Breines & Chen 2012; Wohl et al. 2010; Neff · **high** | One supportive-tactical line + immediate redirect: *"Everyone resets. Next rep: leave the room."* The common-humanity beat matters for a 19yo who thinks he's uniquely broken. |
| **Urges crest and fade** if you don't act | Marlatt urge-surfing; Bowen 2014 · **medium** (15-min is heuristic, *not* measured) | Keep the 15-min full-screen container. Frame: *"Ride it out — urges crest and pass."* Avoid false-precise "exactly 15 minutes." |
| **Acute exercise immediately cuts cravings** | Taylor et al. 2007; 2012 meta-analyses · **high** | "20 pushups" holds up. Offer the fatigued athlete options: *"20 pushups OR a hard 2-min effort OR step outside and move."* |
| **Public/verified monitoring + a referee** beats announcing | Harkin 2016; Giné/Karlan/Zinman 2010; StickK; Cialdini · **high** | The partner-text is the single highest-leverage feature. Make it **one tap** (message pre-filled, partner pre-selected). Strengthen toward a referee who confirms receipt + a pre-written line: *"You told [name] you'd text before, not after."* |
| **Loss aversion → "quit moment," not "restart"** | Kahneman & Tversky 1979; Duolingo data · **medium** | A reset must never render a stark **0** as the dominant number. Make the never-resetting **lifetime** total + WIN counter dominant; the current-run clock secondary. *"New run started — day 1"* beside *"Lifetime clean: 142 days."* |
| **Mental accounting — segregated piles** | Thaler 1985/1999 · **high** | Keep recovery and money as separate, labeled, only-grow piles. Each survived urge = its own discrete logged win (*"Urge outlasted — 38 total"*). |

**⚠️ Open decision — recovery buffer / streak-freeze.** The Duolingo data (~48%
longer persistence with a freeze) argues for a buffer so one slip near a milestone
doesn't visually erase the run. But a buffer that *hides* a real reset collides
with this app's honesty value. **This is your call, not mine to silently build.**

## 3 · Sleep & the athlete — wake/bedtime rules, the nap

> **Top move:** anchor on a consistent **wake** time, not bedtime. Sell it as a
> performance lever for Fresno State, not as discipline.

| Principle | Source · confidence | In the app |
|---|---|---|
| **Sleep extension makes college athletes measurably faster** (~0.7s sprint, age 19.4) | Mah et al. 2011 (Stanford) · **high** | Surface one tactical line tying sleep → the track: *"Banked sleep made college runners ~0.7s faster (Mah 2011). This is training too."* |
| **Phone-out-of-bedroom = CBT-I stimulus control** (not "blue light") | Bootzin 1972; Jansson-Fröjmark 2024 · **high** | Frame as *"Bed = sleep only. Phone out of the room."* Doubles as a porn-recovery guardrail — non-negotiable on two fronts. |
| **Pre-sleep screen use** delays sleep; engagement/timing > wavelength | Heo 2017; NSF 2024 · **high** | Don't add a blue-light-filter toggle as a substitute. The rule is "phone away," never "phone with filter on." |
| **Consistent wake time** is the stronger circadian anchor; regularity > duration | Windred 2024 (UK Biobank, ~30% lower mortality); Phillips 2017 · **high** | Make 6:45 immovable, let bedtime float. *"Lock the wake; let bedtime find it."* Same time 7 days incl. weekends. Don't add a rigid bedtime alarm as a co-equal rule. |
| **Morning light within ~30 min** phase-advances the clock | Crowley & Eastman 2015 · **high** | The morning run already delivers it — make that explicit. Add *"get outside within 30 min of waking"* on run-rest days. |
| **Nap 20–30 min, before ~4pm** after hard sessions | Lastella et al. · **high** | *"Nap 20-30 min, before 4pm — long enough to recover, short enough to wake up sharp."* A late/2-hr nap is the main thing that sabotages the wake anchor. |
| **Grace window protects the habit** | Lally 2010; van Timmeren 2022; Marlatt · **high** | 6:45–~7:15 still reads **DONE** (forgiving clock, firm cue). A late wake is logged data, never red MISSED, never a streak-break. |

**Don't build:** a green/red sleep-hours duration tracker — it re-imports the
all-or-nothing failure framing the app exists to avoid.

## 4 · Focus & deep work — the sprint timer

> **Top move:** the running sprint is a full-screen single-purpose **cockpit**
> that hides everything else — directly attacking the two best-evidenced focus
> killers (attention residue, phone presence).

| Principle | Source · confidence | In the app |
|---|---|---|
| **Attention residue** — switching before "closed" degrades the next task | Leroy 2009 · **high** | Sprint screen hides streak/income/journal/checklist + notifications while running. |
| **Phone-presence brain drain** — real but **small / contested** | Ward et al. 2017; Böttger 2023 meta · **high** (effect is small) | Optional low-key *"Phone in the next room"* suggestion at start. A nudge, **not** a gate, honest that it's small. |
| **25 min is arbitrary** (a kitchen timer, c.1987) | Cirillo; Kleitman BRAC · **medium** | Don't claim "science says 25." Tooltip: *"Pick the length you can hold. 20-25 is a starting point, not a law."* |
| **Mid-subtask interruptions cost most** | Adamczyk & Bailey 2004; Mark 2008 · **high** | Build a **capped** *"Finish this thought (+5)"* button (1–2 per sprint) so stops land at boundaries. Soft *"Wrap it up — 30s"* at zero, not a hard cut. |
| **Forced breaks beat self-regulated** ones | Biwer et al. 2023 · **high** | Default to an automatic short bounded break after each sprint — prompted even when he feels fine. No easy "skip break / keep going." |
| **Restorative breaks = off-screen** (nature/soft fascination) | Kaplan ART 1995; Berman 2008 · **high** | Break screen: *"Step outside, look far, walk it off. Skip the feed — screens don't recharge focus."* Reuse the urge-protocol "get outside." |
| **Writing the next step frees attention** (Zeigarnik itself is weak) | Masicampo & Baumeister 2011 · **medium** | End-of-sprint one-tap *"Next sprint starts here: ___"*, shown at the top of the next start. |
| **Goal-gradient** on the 6-dot target | Kivetz et al. 2006 · **high** | *"X to go"* near the end. Past 6 → quiet bonus dots toward the weekly tally (no confetti). |

## 5 · Streaks, money & accountability

| Principle | Source · confidence | In the app |
|---|---|---|
| **Goal-gradient + endowed progress** | Kivetz et al. 2006 · **high** | $3500/mo + 6/day shown as a bar with **real** ground already covered + nearest-finish sub-goals. (Endowed progress must be *real*, never faked.) |
| **Mental accounting** — labeled, segregated, growing balances | Thaler 1985/1999 · **high** | Lifetime income "pile" = permanent, only-grows, separate from the monthly target. Mirror for lifetime days-clean + WINs. Don't merge money + recovery into one score. |
| **Commitment devices w/ a referee** beat announcements | Giné/Karlan/Zinman 2010; StickK; Cialdini · **high** | Strengthen the partner-text from "into the void" → verified two-way commitment + a pre-written, freely-chosen line. |
| **Announcing identity goals REDUCES follow-through** | Gollwitzer et al. 2009 · **high** | **Don't** build features that broadcast identity-level goals ("becoming a disciplined man") to an audience — that saps the striving. Keep accountability to concrete verifiable *actions*, shared only with the 1–2 partners. Debrief stays private. |

## Deliberately **NOT** building (the dark-side guardrails)

Gamification has a documented dark side — anxiety, compulsive checking, and it
demotivates exactly the low-motivation users recovery apps serve (Hammedi 2021;
SilverCloud 2022 · medium). So, on purpose, **no**:

- confetti / badges / points / leaderboards
- variable or random "reward" reveals (slot-machine mechanics)
- red **"YOU LOST YOUR STREAK"** interstitials or any shame-on-miss copy
- escalating nag notifications / engagement-bait
- a public feed or identity broadcast
- a green/red sleep-hours tracker

Notifications stay few and action-cued (10:15 phone-out, morning protocol).
Success is measured by real outcomes — clean days, sprints, income, miles —
**not time-in-app.**

§0 · The ruling principle

LOCKED IN is the doctrine; Telemetry is the instrument. The perps-terminal aesthetic is a skin over the same psychology — the user now reads himself as a market instead of a pool, but the market must obey the pool's rules. Where the terminal look and the psychology collide, the psychology wins, every time. This is the tiebreaker for every ruling in §3.

Two laws from toneEngine.js are marked binding in the code itself and bind this spec too:

  1. Post-slip copy is one voice for all profiles. Loss framing protects a streak; it never indicts a broken one. No design surface may re-introduce profile branching after a slip.
  2. Severity shifts register, not volume. Escalation changes how a line reads, never how many surfaces or notifications fire. guardianEngine caps notifications at one per app-day; no visual pattern may imply more.

This is a safety property, not just an architecture: generated copy can never smuggle in a UI the hard rules haven't audited — a new surface requires a new registry entry, which requires a §7 row and a hard-rules pass.

§3 · Conflict rulings

Each ruling is final unless a ⚠ item in §10 contradicts it when the missing code is reconciled.

R1Gold rule vs. terminal green — the accent-discipline law
Conflict: LOCKED IN permits its accent in exactly 3 places; a perps terminal splashes green everywhere. Ruling: green-as-semantic-direction (--pos) is data and may appear wherever data moves up. Green-as-reward (--accent fills, glows, buttons) keeps the scarcity law: the day's anchor (DeepWorkTimer), the single primary action per surface, the active tab underline, and HELP NOW. Nothing else earns an accent fill. Why: if the accent is everywhere, attention has no price; the anchor block must remain the brightest thing he owns.
R2Perps red vs. "never a red loss state"
Conflict: strict green-up/red-down semantics vs. the founding ban on red failure states. Ruling: --neg is a market direction, legal only on measures of external conditions (screen time up, HRV down, a late delta). It is forbidden on: the slip close screen, missed schedule rows, any streak number, any sentence whose subject is the user. A missed block is muted and dashed; a reset renders in accent-on-dark calm ("data, not verdict"); urge.slipped never inherits drift styling. Why: red on the self is shame; red on a ticker is weather. The type system enforces the difference: verdicts about the person never take tone props.
R3Faith content in a metrics app
Conflict: Telemetry quantifies everything; the doctrine forbids scoring the spiritual. Ruling: spiritual items may never appear as tickers, progress bars, KPI cells, or verdicted schedule rows. The faith module is opt-in (survey + settings), renders only inside MIND as an un-scored card (verse · reading position · a cue line), and carries the framing "offered, not performed." Three violations existed in the kit's demo data (FAITH ticker with +15%, a Scripture-streak progress bar, a verdicted scripture block) — corrected in the kit, see §9. Why: the un-scored surface is the doctrine's proof that the app measures behavior, not the soul.
R4Streak clock vs. profile psychology — composition adapts, widgets don't
Conflict: the old design had one sacred scoreboard; toneEngine proves the same number motivates one profile and wounds another. Ruling: the widget set is shared; the forge reorders the deck per profile (§5) and toneEngine picks the framing. Day-0 override for all profiles: the lifetime StatRow is auto-promoted to slot 1 of TODAY so a reset never renders 0 as the dominant number ⚠ guardianEngine post-reset deck rule, verify. Why: this is the generative payoff — server-driven UI lets layout itself be therapeutic, not just copy.
R5The hold gesture — who pays the friction in OUTLAST IT
Conflict: LOCKED IN's press-and-hold is ceremonial attestation (Handover); Telemetry's brief puts a 1.2s hold-to-surrender inside the urge override. Ruling: both stand, because both charge the costly path: in the evening seal, the hold attests; in OUTLAST IT, the hold is the price of giving in — staying costs nothing (releasing early cancels), surrendering costs 1.2 deliberate seconds with a slow L→R sweep and no color flash. On completion the screen goes to the one-voice urge.slipped close: calm, muted, next-rep. Why: friction inversion is the product's signature move; the terminal skin just renders the price as a filling bar.
R6Fixed tabs + sub-views vs. server-driven tabs
Conflict: the old IA hid depth under Morning sub-views; the new shell has no nested navigation. Ruling: depth arrives as forged tabs, never pushed routes: the weekly Sunday Debrief becomes a server-pushed REVIEW tab on Sundays; Sprint's cockpit is the DeepWorkTimer widget going fullscreen while running (hides deck, tab strip stays); Streak's calendar and Money/Train detail are TRENDS compositions. Back = tab switch; the strip never exceeds ~5 tabs ⚠ forge tab budget, verify. Why: one navigation idea, zero hamburger drawers, and the calm three-face default survives — extra faces exist only on the days they earn their place.
R7"BEARISH" about a person
Conflict: MarketSentimentWidget scores sentiment 0–100; a low score could read as a verdict on him. Ruling: Internal Markets reads conditions — sleep, screen load, schedule drift — never the person. The sentiment score maps from guardianEngine severity; sub-40 copy uses weather framing ("conditions are stacked", from drift.critical), and the widget label never contains "you". WATCH renders InsightCard tone:"warn", CRITICAL tone:"neg" — legal under R2 because a forecast is weather, not verdict; the post-slip screen may never reuse this styling. Why: toneEngine already writes these lines as forecasts; the visual layer must not re-personalize them.
R8PWA doctrine on a Capacitor native shell
Ruling: native capabilities are adopted only where they serve the doctrine: real safe-area insets; local notifications carrying warn.notification (one per day, theme-registered titles); a single soft haptic tick when a hold completes (never celebratory patterns); HealthKit read-only for biometrics. Forbidden regardless of platform: app icon badges, red dots, streak widgets on the lock screen (a reset would broadcast), push escalation. Why: the platform got stronger; the notification budget didn't.

§8 · Overlay flows

OUTLAST IT — STATE MACHINE
  1. Enter — HELP NOW tap (or shortcut/intercept ⚠ triggers). Full-screen, calm dark regardless of theme. 15:00 clock auto-starts. Subtitle = urge.open per profile.
  2. Steps — fixed, ordered, one visible at a time: leave the room → 20 pushups → text your partner (one tap, pre-filled — danger routes to a person) → get outside → ride it out.
  3. Exit A · outlasted — clock ends or he closes having stayed: urge.survived, logged as a WIN, OUTLASTED pile +1. No confetti; the number climbing is the ceremony.
  4. Exit B · surrender — hold-to-surrender: 1.2s bar sweeps L→R, muted (not red, not accent); releasing early cancels at no cost (R5). On completion: the one-voice urge.slipped close, then the day-0 deck override (§5).
SYNC & REFACTOR (EXAMEN SEAL)

Press → confirm sheet framed by step.commit ("Seal it…") → ceremonial hold → bar flips to queued (accent border). The ledger seals, the forge rebuilds tomorrow's deck overnight. One seal per day; re-opening the app after sealing shows the queued state, not a second ask.

THE ONE NOTIFICATION

warn.notification, pre-window, native local. Title register per theme (UPPERCASE / soft / lowercase). Body always ends in the one 60-second action. Never more than one per app-day; no badges ever (R8).

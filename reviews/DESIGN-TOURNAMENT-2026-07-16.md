## behavioral — 91/100

This is the rare product where the relapse-prevention literature is load-bearing design, not garnish: the split ledger keeps a lifetime pile a reset cannot touch, post-slip copy is a single compassionate voice enforced in code and tests, the urge screen counts up (survival framing), forecasts rather than declares victory, keeps 988 call-and-text on all three phases, and the slip is logged in silence — no buzz, no red, no verdict. What keeps it out of the 95s is choreography at the moment of peak dysregulation: a strictly-ordered protocol that can dead-end on an impossible step, an arming label that reads like a threat ("closes in 0:59"), and a crisis entry point wearing the same shield icon as a paywalled feature.

**Strengths:**
- The split-ledger streak model is textbook Abstinence Violation Effect mitigation: lifetime clean days and urges-outlasted dominate every surface, so a reset zeroes the run honestly without ever rendering a stark 0 as the only number (deck hero, Pulse grid, night-page bezel all verified).
- Post-slip state is clinically right: one voice for all motivational profiles ('Logged. The clock restarts; the work does not.'), no red anywhere, the crisis line persists on the slip close screen, and the code comments bind it to Randles & Tracy with tests enforcing it.
- Urge-protocol choreography shows unusual craft: ride clock counts UP, 'I stayed' arms at 60s so the pile stays earned, the ✕ escape logs nothing, the pre-crest copy forecasts rather than declares ('you're likely through the worst'), and success gets the seal haptic while the slip hold gets deliberate silence.
- Guardian framing is weather-report, not judge: 'inside your stated danger window' vs 'historical' is switched on actual evidence in guardianEngine.js so a survey prior never fabricates a failure history (nocebo guard), and notifications are capped at one per app-day.
- Faith and recovery content is quiet and consensual — opt-in module, 'Offered, not performed,' never a metric — and day 0 is an invitation ('The book opens · Day one'), not a deficit.

## monetization — 87/100

This is some of the most ethically clean subscription design I've judged: the two-column contract makes the free/paid boundary literal, the auto-renew disclosure sits adjacent to the CTA and adapts to the selected plan and lapsed state, crisis surfaces are never gated, and the paywall only appears when the user reaches for coach value. The gaps are operational rather than moral — a subscriber has no in-app way to see or manage their plan, the lapsed user is still shown a "7-day free" promise on every gate, and the locked Guardian screen double-fires the sell. Nothing here would embarrass me at launch, but three of these must be fixed before submission.

**Strengths:**
- The paywall's two-column 'THE BOOK · FREE / THE COACH · 7-DAY FREE' contract is the best free-vs-paid story articulation I've seen at this price point — no free feature is dressed up as paid (DailyBriefing stays free and says READ), and 'Your book stays free. Always.' is repeated at every gate.
- Compliance and honesty around the CTA are exemplary: 3.1.2 auto-renew disclosure directly under the buy button, wording that adapts per selected plan AND per lapsed state ('If your free week was used, $X is charged now'), 44px Restore/Privacy/Terms targets, StoreKit-localized prices, honest annual anchoring ($3.33/mo billed once) with no fake strikethroughs or countdowns.
- Gate placement earns trust before asking: no paywall in onboarding or the tour (one honest mention on tour page 5), the night page / urge protocol / 988 / slip logging are never gated or upsold — exactly the standard a recovery user base demands.
- The personalized paywall header (personalLine() in Paywall.jsx) reads back only the user's own survey answers and returns null rather than inventing — personalization without manipulation.
- The milestone share loop (LiveDeck.jsx SHARE_MILESTONES) asks exactly once per milestone, persists dismissals, skips day-0, and back-fills passed milestones on import — a growth surface with self-respect.

## brand-voice — 84/100

The book has a real voice — "dictate the day," "rule off," "the book opens · Day one," "Your book is yours. The coach is hired." is genuinely ownable language no competitor is using, and the slip/crisis register (one compassionate voice for all profiles, "Logged · the book stays open," call-and-text 988 kept on every phase) is the best copywriting in the app. But the brand still speaks four dialects: the book (core surfaces), a trading floor ("EXEC," "read of your tape," "ENGAGED %"), an ops terminal ("DRIFT SENTINEL," "OPERATOR," "ENGINE TYPE," "PROTOCOL INTELLIGENCE"), and raw engineer-speak shipped to users ("phone build," "HealthKit is native-only," "the HEALTH surface"). The first screen a user ever sees speaks the wrong dialect ("how aggressive the protocol starts," "NOTHING SHIPPED"), and the sacred word "seal" — reserved for the user's own commitments — is reused as the paywall's lock.

**Strengths:**
- Sensitive-moment copy is exemplary: post-slip is one profile-blind compassionate voice (toneEngine.js hard rule 1), the slip screen says "Logged · the book stays open," and Call 988 / text 988 persist on the active ride, the stayed screen, AND the post-slip screen.
- The tour codifies the grammar in six pages — "This is a ledger, not a feed," "The page prints what you dictated," "dictate, execute, rule off. The book does the remembering" — so the metaphor is taught, not assumed.
- The paywall is the most on-brand monetization copy I've seen in this category: "Your book is yours. The coach is hired," "YOUR BOOK STAYS FREE. ALWAYS," free column lists "The night page & 988" — crisis access explicitly named as never-paid.
- Ownable lexicon: "days on the book," "rule off the day," "the night page," "dictate the day," "offered, not performed" — distinctive, consistent across all three skins, and defensible against generic discipline apps.
- Day-0 hero ("THE BOOK OPENS — Day one · Your first entry goes here") reframes the emptiest, riskiest moment as an invitation instead of a zero — exactly right for a shame-sensitive user base.

## data-viz — 82/100

The quiet surfaces genuinely earn the "telemetry" name: the heat sheet is a real table, the EnergyTrendLine is honest ruled-ledger work with margin annotations instead of glow, and the Guardian's drift list labels NO DATA instead of inventing a score — that discipline is rare. But the flagship Daily Briefing strip undercuts it: it prints "ENGAGED 0%" and "IMPACT 0/4" before anything has happened, and its CARDS denominator drifts (0/5 on the deck, 0/11 on the night capture, same day at 17:20) — a self-referential app-engagement vanity metric with an unstable denominator, front and center on the hero surface. Fix the briefing strip, the color-only late/skip glyphs, and the min-max autoscale, and this is a 90+ system.

**Strengths:**
- Empty states are honest everywhere it matters: Readiness renders a dash + "the band waits for real data", Trends shows a dashed NO ENERGY DATA YET slot at fixed height, and Guardian vectors print explicit NO DATA rows instead of fabricated scores.
- EnergyTrendLine (widgets/EnergyTrendLine.jsx) is real Tufte work: carbon-ink line on hairline grid, avg as a single brighter hairline, peak/low/now as small marks with figures in the margin, no gradient fill, no verdict color on the body — and low reads amber, never red.
- The heat sheet is a genuine table — mono tabular time column, ruled dividers, ◆ confined to the left margin as the page's only accent, executed-count in the header, ink-applied/ink-withheld glyphs instead of color verdicts — with full VoiceOver labels carrying what the glyphs encode.
- guardianEngine.js is honest under the hood: weighted vectors with evidence flags, CRITICAL gated on ≥2 observed-evidence vectors, band hysteresis so the verdict doesn't flap, and day-0 explicitly returns "first day on the book" instead of a risk flag — a no-zero-data-verdict machine.
- KpiGrid's trial-balance strip (hairline column rules, no tile boxes) and the WeekGrid's label/figure/bar/seal stack are dense, aligned, and unit-consistent — high data-ink ratio for a 390pt viewport.

**NOW:**
- **Daily Briefing prints zero-data verdicts with an unstable denominator** — On the deck (06) the LIVE briefing reads IMPACT 0/4 · ENGAGED 0% · CARDS 0/5 while the body admits "Nothing on the book yet"; the night capture (09), same day, same 17:20, shows CARDS 0/11 — the denominator counts whatever widgets happen to have rendered (engagement.js registers on render), so the figure changes as you navigate. "ENGAGED 0%" is a percentage of an empty set — a failing grade before the day starts. Render an em-dash for ENGAGED and IMPACT until the first read/post exists, and freeze CARDS' denominator to the dealt deck at forge time, not cumulative rendered widgets. _[Deck · Daily Briefing strip, src/lib/engagement.js]_
- **Heat-sheet 'late' and 'skip' glyphs differ by color alone** — In widgets.jsx StateGlyph, late renders '–' in text-warn and skip renders '–' in text-muted — identical shape, color-only distinction (and pending's faint '—' is a near-identical dash). This breaks the file's own stated rule that "the shape carries the fact; color would carry the verdict" and is illegible to color-blind users. Give late a distinct mark (e.g. ✓ with the amber time it already gets, or a half-circle ◔) and keep skip as the muted dash. _[Deck · heat sheet (ScheduleMatrix), src/components/widgets.jsx StateGlyph]_

## interaction — 81/100

The written contract is the best I've reviewed at this scale — four motion verbs, one ease, a one-haptic grammar that never buzzes a shortfall, a 1.2s hold with a real accessible equivalent, and a 60-second arm on "I stayed" so the lifetime pile can't be inflated. But the ceremony lives mostly in the spec and the body, not the eye: the visual twin of the commit haptic (haptic-beat) is dead code, sheets and surface switches are hard cuts with zero motion, the commit hold looks identical to the slip hold, and INITIALIZE — "the first seal" — is gesturally indistinguishable from Next. This is a 95-point grammar wired at about 70%.

**Strengths:**
- The haptic grammar is genuinely Rams-grade and recovery-safe: one SUCCESS tap on commits only (post a row, DeepWork sealed, I stayed, INITIALIZE, rule-off), hard silence on slips/misses, warmSeal() preloading the bridge on pointer-down so the tap has zero latency, and no fake vibration fallback on web.
- HoldButton is a model commitment control: 1.2s fill with free early release, pointer-leave cancel, touch-action none, plus a real WCAG 2.1.1 path (keyboard hold, two-tap confirm with aria-live) — deliberate friction without excluding anyone.
- The Outlast ceremony inverts weight correctly: staying arms only after 60s of real ride (announced via role=status), the ✕ escape logs nothing, surrendering costs a silent 1.2s hold, and the count-up clock's breath animation stops once the crest passes — motion communicating state, not decorating it.
- A written, enforced motion ceiling: four named verbs, one cubic-bezier, tokenized durations (--dur-quick/calm/pulse/seal/stamp), every skin resolving glow tokens to none, and a global prefers-reduced-motion kill switch.
